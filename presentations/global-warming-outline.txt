Global Warming (What it is & why it matters)
	Global warming is the long-term rise in Earth’s average temperature, driven mainly by human-caused greenhouse gas emissions.
	Key greenhouse gases: CO₂, CH₄ (methane), N₂O (nitrous oxide).
	Why it matters: shifts climate patterns, raises sea levels, increases extreme weather risks.

Main Causes (Human drivers)
	Burning fossil fuels (electricity, heat, transport) → largest CO₂ source
	Deforestation and land-use change → less carbon stored in forests/soils
	Agriculture & livestock → methane and nitrous oxide
	Industry & waste → additional emissions

Evidence & Current Impacts
	Rising global average temperatures and more frequent heatwaves
	Melting glaciers/ice sheets; shrinking Arctic sea ice
	Sea-level rise affecting coastal communities
	More intense rainfall events and drought risk in many regions
	Ecosystem stress (coral bleaching, species range shifts)

Risks Ahead (If warming continues)
	Higher probability of extreme heat, wildfire conditions, and water stress
	Food security challenges (crop yield variability, fisheries disruption)
	Health impacts: heat illness, air quality degradation, spread of some diseases
	Economic losses: infrastructure damage, productivity decline, insurance strain

Solutions (What works)
	Clean electricity: solar/wind + grid upgrades + storage
	Electrify transport & buildings: EVs, public transit, heat pumps, efficiency
	Cut methane fast: oil/gas leak control, waste management, improved agriculture practices
	Protect & restore nature: forests, wetlands, soil carbon
	Policy + finance + innovation + individual actions together
